About

I am an Associate Professor of Economics at the University of Lorraine, specializing in environmental and natural resource economics. My research explores environmental, energy, agricultural, and animal health challenges, with a particular emphasis on the heterogeneity of individuals and territories. I am interested in how economic behaviors and public policies influence sustainable resource management and contribute to addressing contemporary ecological and health challenges.

In addition to research, I teach courses in environmental economics, econometrics, and quantitative methods. I am actively involved in supervising students and engaging with interdisciplinary projects at the crossroads of environmental and health issues.

Thank you for visiting my page. If you would like to get in touch, please feel free to contact me by email.

Research interests

  • Public finance & fiscal federalism
  • Spatial interactions
  • Energy & climate
  • Animal health economics
